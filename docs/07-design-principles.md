# Stage 07 - Design Principles

## 1. Premium Through Control

Premium should come from proportion, spacing, hierarchy, and restraint.

Do:

- Use disciplined grids.
- Keep proof close to claims.
- Make CTAs clear but calm.

Avoid:

- Decorative luxury signals without purpose.
- Oversized buttons.
- Loud gradients.
- Ornate event styling.

## 2. Personal Before Corporate

This is Mahnaz's site, not ProVia Events or EventAgent.

Do:

- Use a strong portrait.
- Make the name and role clear.
- Explain related brands as context.

Avoid:

- Letting ProVia Events or EventAgent dominate the header, hero, or navigation.

## 3. Structure As A Visual Motif

The site is about bringing order to complex event environments.

Do:

- Use columns, timelines, proof rows, and structured cards.
- Keep repeated modules consistent.

Avoid:

- Chaotic collage layouts.
- Random decorative flourishes.
- Uneven card heights without a reason.

## 4. Warmth Without Softness

The brand should feel hospitable, but not casual or sentimental.

Do:

- Use warm neutrals.
- Use human photography.
- Keep language and visuals composed.

Avoid:

- Pastel-heavy wedding mood.
- Excessive blush/gold luxury cues.
- Party imagery as the main visual language.

## 5. Proof Before Persuasion

The design should make verified credibility easy to scan.

Do:

- Promote metrics.
- Create selected experience cards.
- Use factual labels.

Avoid:

- Testimonial sections before testimonials are approved.
- Logo strips without approved logos.
- Decorative trust badges.

## 6. Mobile First Impression Matters

Mobile must communicate who Mahnaz is and what to do next immediately.

Do:

- Keep H1 readable.
- Show primary CTA early.
- Stack proof cleanly.

Avoid:

- Portraits that push the CTA too far down.
- Multi-column compression.
- Horizontal overflow.
