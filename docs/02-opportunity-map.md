# Stage 02 - Opportunity Map

## Core Opportunity

Position Mahnaz as the named senior operator behind structured, calm, high-stakes event strategy.

Competitors commonly claim beauty, experience, creativity, scale, or seamless execution. Mahnaz can own a more specific lane:

> The senior event strategist who understands the room, the guest experience, the vendors, the timeline, and the system required to keep everything moving.

## Messaging Whitespace

### 1. Structure Over Generic Luxury

Competitors often lean on luxury language. Mahnaz can use structure as the premium signal:

- Calm planning.
- Clear responsibilities.
- Vendor alignment.
- Guest-flow awareness.
- Timeline control.
- Hospitality-informed decisions.

### 2. Personal Judgment Over Agency Breadth

Many competitors feel like agencies. Mahnaz's site should feel like direct access to a senior event strategist.

Opportunity:

- "Work with Mahnaz" rather than "hire our team."
- Make her judgment, experience, and point of view visible.
- Keep ProVia Events as the operating context, not the hero brand.

### 3. Hospitality Plus Technology

Few event-planning competitors can credibly combine:

- Hospitality operations.
- Event planning.
- Business development.
- ERP/software leadership.
- EventAgent strategy.

This gives Mahnaz a distinctive "event systems" credibility lane.

### 4. Calm Authority For High-Stakes Rooms

The strongest emotional promise is not "your event will be magical." It is:

- Your guests will know where to go.
- Your vendors will know what matters.
- Your program will stay controlled.
- Your team will have a senior operator thinking ahead.

## UX Whitespace

### 1. Simpler Than An Agency Site

Use fewer pages and fewer service paths than competitors. The site should help visitors quickly answer:

- Who is Mahnaz?
- Why trust her?
- What kinds of events/consulting does she support?
- How do I book a consultation?

### 2. Proof-Led Personal Homepage

Use proof metrics early:

- 10+ years event planning and hospitality experience.
- 18 years total professional experience.
- 100+ events managed.
- 800+ attendee event experience.
- 750+ guest gala experience.
- 2,000+ hospitality software clients through prior business leadership.

### 3. EventAgent Context Without Product Confusion

Create a small section that explains how Mahnaz's event experience informs structured event systems and EventAgent strategy. Avoid turning the page into a product pitch.

## Trust-Building Opportunities

- Professional portrait with confident, warm styling.
- LinkedIn visible near bio and footer.
- Selected work grouped by event type rather than a generic portfolio.
- "How Mahnaz thinks about events" section to convey method.
- Public proof numbers with restrained copy.
- ProVia Events affiliation.
- EventAgent strategy affiliation once exact title is approved.

## Content Opportunities

High-value future content:

- "What makes an event feel calm to guests?"
- "Why guest flow matters before the first speech begins."
- "The hidden operations behind a successful gala."
- "Event strategy versus event decoration."
- "What hospitality taught me about business events."
- "How technology is changing event coordination."

These could support SEO and LinkedIn authority later, but blog/CMS is not launch-critical for the first version.

## Design Opportunities

Best direction:

- Editorial personal profile.
- Premium but warm color system.
- Strong typography and calm spacing.
- One excellent portrait.
- Minimal but meaningful motion, if used.
- Proof cards that feel like credentials, not sales badges.

Avoid:

- Overly ornate wedding styling.
- Dark luxury cliches.
- High-energy corporate confetti/event imagery.
- Software dashboard visuals as the main brand cue.

## Strategic Implications For Stage 03

Stage 03 should develop positioning that:

- Leads with Mahnaz's senior personal credibility.
- Keeps event strategy and execution tied together.
- Uses hospitality and technology as differentiators.
- Uses ProVia Events and EventAgent as proof anchors, not competing brands.
- Makes Calendly consultation the clear commercial next step.

## Strategic Implications For Stage 04

Stage 04 should keep the funnel focused:

- Primary offer: Event Strategy Consultation.
- Supporting paths: corporate/business events, luxury/private events, hospitality consulting, speaking/emcee if approved.
- Secondary action: LinkedIn connection.
- Avoid a broad service catalog that duplicates ProVia Events.

## Strategic Implications For Stage 05

Stage 05 should likely prefer a lean sitemap:

- Home
- About
- Experience
- Event Strategy
- Contact / Book

Potential additions only if justified:

- Speaking & Emcee
- Hospitality Consulting
- Insights

## Strategic Implications For Stage 06

Copy should be:

- Specific enough to feel credible.
- Warm enough to feel personal.
- Senior enough to support corporate/luxury clients.
- Operational enough to differentiate.
- Careful enough to avoid unapproved claims.
