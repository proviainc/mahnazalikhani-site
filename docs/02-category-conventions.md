# Stage 02 - Category Conventions

## Category Map

Mahnaz's site touches these categories:

- Personal brand for a senior event professional.
- Toronto event planner/event management.
- Corporate and business event strategy.
- Luxury/private event coordination.
- Hospitality consulting.
- Event operations and event-tech strategy.

## Table Stakes

Visitors will expect:

- Clear identity: who Mahnaz is and what she does.
- Event categories she supports.
- Proof of experience.
- A professional photo.
- CTA to book or contact.
- LinkedIn or another credibility trail.
- Selected work or notable events.
- A short but credible story.
- A sense of style, taste, and professionalism.

## Common Website Patterns

Most event planning websites use:

- Large visual hero.
- Services cards.
- Portfolio/gallery.
- Testimonials.
- Process steps.
- Client logos or media mentions.
- Contact form or inquiry form.
- Blog/guides for SEO.

Most personal-brand consultant sites use:

- Strong point of view.
- Founder bio.
- Offer paths.
- Proof strip.
- Speaking/media/authority signals.
- Book a call CTA.

Most event-tech websites use:

- Problem/solution framing.
- Workflow diagrams.
- Platform benefits.
- Feature lists.
- Product demo or partner CTA.
- Language around centralization, timelines, vendors, approvals, and communication.

## Buyer Expectations

Corporate and business event buyers expect:

- Professionalism.
- Budget and timeline confidence.
- Understanding of stakeholders and brand goals.
- Operational reliability.
- Evidence of managing complexity.

Luxury private event buyers expect:

- Taste.
- Discretion.
- Calm communication.
- Sensitivity to guest experience.
- Confidence that personal details will be handled well.

Hospitality consulting buyers expect:

- Practical operational knowledge.
- Process improvement thinking.
- Experience with staff, service flow, guest experience, and cost control.

EventAgent-related visitors expect:

- Credibility in real-world event operations.
- Evidence that strategy is grounded in actual event execution.
- Clear separation between Mahnaz's personal credibility and the product itself.

## Trust Signals That Matter

High-value trust signals:

- Specific event metrics.
- Named event types.
- Event scale.
- Role clarity.
- Hospitality background.
- Technology/business background.
- Professional portrait.
- LinkedIn.
- ProVia Events affiliation.
- EventAgent affiliation with approved wording.

Lower-value or risky trust signals:

- Generic stock event photos.
- Unverified testimonials.
- Overstated luxury language.
- Long lists of services without proof.
- Claims like "best," "flawless," or "world-class."

## Conversion Conventions

Common CTAs:

- Book a consultation.
- Request a quote.
- Plan an event.
- Contact us.
- Schedule a strategy session.

Best-fit CTA for Mahnaz:

- Book an Event Strategy Consultation.

Secondary CTA:

- Connect on LinkedIn.

## Messaging Conventions To Use Carefully

Useful if made specific:

- Event strategy.
- Guest experience.
- Vendor coordination.
- Run-of-show or agenda flow.
- Hospitality operations.
- Event-day execution.
- Structured planning.

Avoid or tightly control:

- Unforgettable.
- Flawless.
- Magical.
- Bespoke.
- Premier.
- Stress-free.
- Every detail.

## Design Conventions

Expected:

- Premium typography.
- Strong photo direction.
- Calm spacing.
- Clear hierarchy.
- Mobile-first CTA access.
- Selected proof, not dense resume copy.

Opportunity:

- Blend editorial personal-brand design with operational clarity.
- Use restrained visual polish instead of generic luxury decoration.
- Let proof metrics carry authority where visual assets are still limited.
