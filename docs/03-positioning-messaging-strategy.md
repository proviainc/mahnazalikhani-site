# Stage 03 - Positioning & Messaging Strategy

## Positioning Summary

Mahnaz Alikhani is a senior event strategist and hospitality-minded operator who helps corporate, luxury, hospitality, and business-event teams bring structure, calm, and control to high-stakes gatherings.

She should be understood as more than an event planner. Her strongest position is the named senior professional who understands the room, the guest experience, the vendors, the timeline, and the operating system behind an event that works.

## Sharp Positioning Statement

Mahnaz Alikhani helps high-stakes event teams turn complex moving parts into calm, structured, guest-ready experiences, backed by deep event execution, hospitality consulting, and event-technology strategy.

## Ideal Customer Lens

Primary audience:

- Corporate and business event clients who need senior judgment, operational clarity, and stakeholder confidence.
- Luxury private event clients who need discretion, taste, and calm coordination.
- Hospitality consulting clients who need practical service-flow, staff, guest-experience, and operational improvement thinking.
- Business event organizers who need event strategy, program flow, and event-day control.

What they care about most:

- Will the event feel organized and professional?
- Will guests know where to go and what to expect?
- Will vendors and internal teams stay aligned?
- Will the program, timeline, and room flow stay controlled?
- Can I trust this person with a high-visibility event?

## Category Role

Primary category:

- Personal brand for a senior event strategist.

Supporting categories:

- Event planning and event management.
- Hospitality consulting.
- Luxury and corporate event execution.
- Event operations and event-technology strategy.

The site should not behave like a full agency site. It should behave like a senior professional's credibility platform that routes qualified visitors toward a consultation.

## Core Problem Framing

High-stakes events rarely fail because of one visible detail. They struggle when the invisible system behind the room is weak:

- Guest arrival is unclear.
- Vendors are misaligned.
- Timelines drift.
- Sponsors, hosts, speakers, or VIPs do not know what happens next.
- The client is forced to manage problems instead of being present.

Mahnaz's message should show that successful events require both hospitality and structure: care for the guest experience and control over the operational details.

## Brand Promise

Mahnaz brings calm structure to complex events so hosts, teams, and guests can move through the experience with confidence.

## Objections And Reframes

### "Is this just a personal resume site?"

Reframe:

- This is a direct credibility and consultation path for clients, partners, and collaborators who want Mahnaz's judgment.

### "Should I contact ProVia Events instead?"

Reframe:

- ProVia Events remains the company-service destination. This site helps visitors understand Mahnaz's personal expertise and book a strategy conversation with her.

### "Is she a planner, consultant, emcee, or event-tech strategist?"

Reframe:

- The unifying role is event strategist and operator. Planning, consulting, emcee leadership, and EventAgent strategy all sit under structured event leadership.

### "Can she support premium or corporate work?"

Reframe:

- Use verified proof: event scale, vendor/sponsor coordination, galas, corporate gatherings, hospitality consulting, and business background.

## Voice And Tone

The site should sound:

- Calm
- Specific
- Senior
- Warm
- Practical
- Premium without exaggeration

Avoid:

- Inflated luxury claims.
- "Flawless" and "unforgettable" as core promises.
- Generic event-planner language.
- Product-heavy EventAgent language.
- Overly playful corporate-event energy.
