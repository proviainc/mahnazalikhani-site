# Stage 06 - Copywriting & Content Architecture

## Global Content Principles

The copy system should make Mahnaz's personal credibility clear without turning the site into a resume, a ProVia Events duplicate, or an EventAgent product page.

Write for serious visitors who are validating judgment, trust, fit, and next step:

- Lead with structured event strategy.
- Use verified proof early.
- Explain operational value in plain language.
- Keep ProVia Events and EventAgent as credibility context.
- Make Calendly booking the guided next step.
- Avoid generic event-planning claims and unsupported luxury language.

## Homepage Content Architecture

The homepage should do most of the strategic work:

1. Hero: who Mahnaz is, what she helps with, primary CTA.
2. Proof strip: verified credibility signals.
3. Audience-fit section: corporate/business, luxury private, hospitality consulting, business organizers.
4. Operating philosophy: the event works when the invisible system works.
5. Selected experience: privacy-safe examples and proof metrics.
6. Consultation section: what an Event Strategy Consultation can cover.
7. ProVia Events and EventAgent context: supporting credibility, not competing brands.
8. Final CTA: Calendly and LinkedIn.

## Core Page Briefs

### Home

Primary job:

- Convert trust into consultation interest.

Main question to answer:

- Why should I trust Mahnaz with a high-stakes event or event strategy conversation?

### About

Primary job:

- Explain the person, career arc, and credibility behind the positioning.

Main question to answer:

- What shaped Mahnaz's judgment across hospitality, events, business, and event technology?

### Experience

Primary job:

- Make proof tangible without exposing private details or overbuilding case studies.

Main question to answer:

- Has Mahnaz handled event situations with comparable complexity?

### Event Strategy

Primary job:

- Explain the consultation offer and route high-intent visitors to Calendly.

Main question to answer:

- What can I discuss with Mahnaz, and why is a strategy call the right first step?

## Headline System

Use direct, grounded headlines:

- Structured event strategy for high-stakes gatherings.
- Calm event leadership for rooms that need control.
- Event strategy shaped by hospitality, execution, and systems.
- The event works when the invisible system works.
- Proof from real rooms, real guests, and real moving parts.
- A strategy call for the event before the event day arrives.

Avoid headlines that could fit any competitor:

- Creating unforgettable experiences.
- Elevating every event.
- Bringing your vision to life.
- Flawless execution from start to finish.

## Proof And Trust Placement

Place proof early and repeat it with restraint:

- Homepage proof strip.
- About credentials section.
- Experience page proof cards.
- Event Strategy trust section.

Do not use:

- Testimonials until approved.
- Logos until approved.
- Private event photos until approved.
- Exact EventAgent title until approved.

## Metadata And Search-Snippet Direction

Each launch page needs a unique title, description, H1, and Open Graph summary.

Search goals:

- Name/entity clarity.
- Event strategy consultation clarity.
- Toronto/GTA relevance where natural.
- Accurate ProVia Events and EventAgent relationship.

## Search-Supporting Content Opportunities

Launch should not include a blog, but future content could cover:

- What makes an event feel calm to guests?
- Why guest flow matters before the first speech begins.
- Event strategy versus event decoration.
- The hidden operations behind a successful gala.
- What hospitality teaches about business events.
- How technology is changing event coordination.

## Authenticity Filter Findings

Risky phrases to reject:

- Flawless.
- Stress-free.
- World-class.
- Unforgettable as a primary claim.
- Best.
- Premier.
- Bespoke.
- Every detail.

Preferred replacements:

- Guest arrival.
- Vendor coordination.
- Timeline control.
- Program flow.
- Hospitality judgment.
- Event-day control.
- Structured event strategy.

## Page Pruning Recommendations

Keep launch scope to four pages:

- Home.
- About.
- Experience.
- Event Strategy.

Do not add launch pages for speaking, hospitality consulting, Insights, or selected work detail until there is enough content and business priority.

Avoid repeated sections that restate "Mahnaz brings structure" without adding proof, audience fit, or a next step.

## Missing Content Requirements

Required before design/build quality can be final:

- Approved portrait/photo.
- Exact approved EventAgent public title.
- Decision on whether phone/email should be public.

Useful but not required for launch:

- Approved testimonials.
- Approved event photos.
- Approved logos.
- Speaking/emcee proof.
- Detailed case notes for selected events.
