# Stage 02 - Market & Competitor Research

## Market Context

Mahnaz Alikhani's website sits between four overlapping categories:

- Personal brand for a senior event strategist.
- Toronto/GTA event planning and event management.
- Luxury/private event and gala execution.
- Event operations and event-technology credibility through EventAgent.

The Toronto event market is crowded with polished event planners, corporate event agencies, luxury event designers, and broad event-management companies. Many competitors compete through either visual beauty, scale, brand logos, awards, or "stress-free/flawless" promises. The opportunity for Mahnaz is to avoid sounding like another generic event planner and instead build a personal authority position around structured event strategy, hospitality judgment, guest experience, and operational control.

## Buying Criteria

Corporate, luxury, hospitality, and business-event buyers typically need confidence in:

- Senior judgment under pressure.
- Experience with event scale, guest flow, vendors, sponsors, and live timelines.
- Clear process from strategy to execution.
- Taste and professionalism without excessive trend language.
- Trustworthiness with private or high-stakes rooms.
- Ability to reduce internal workload and prevent event-day chaos.
- Proof that the person has handled comparable events.
- Easy next step for consultation.

For Mahnaz specifically, visitors will also need clarity on how her personal site relates to ProVia Events and EventAgent.

## Competitor Snapshots

### The Concierge Club

Site: `https://www.theconciergeclub.com/`

Positioning centers on premium experiential marketing and corporate events for major brands. The site uses a broad services menu, awards, ownership identifiers, newsletter capture, and a budget-qualifying contact form. Its strongest pattern is commercial scale and brand-facing sophistication.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- Do not try to out-agency an agency.
- Borrow the clarity around event categories and budget-aware qualification later if the funnel expands.
- Avoid generic "unforgettable experiences" language unless paired with sharper proof.

### Rebecca Chan Events

Site: `https://rebeccachan.ca/`

Positioning blends luxury event planning, design-forward corporate events, weddings, media authority, and founder credibility. The site leans heavily on beauty, personality, press, testimonials, and planning content.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- Personal credibility can work well when attached to a visible expert.
- Mahnaz should not over-index on design glamour; her stronger lane is structured execution and operational calm.
- Thought-leadership content could help later, especially around event strategy and hospitality operations.

### Bassett Events

Site: `https://www.bassetteventsinc.com/`

Positioning is high-end luxury event management with prominent client/talent names, testimonials, and an extensive service capability list. The language connects logistics mastery with astonishment.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- The "discipline behind the room" concept is useful.
- Mahnaz should use proof and specificity rather than trying to mimic celebrity/luxury signaling.
- Selected event experience should be concise, not a long agency-style capability inventory.

### Pop Events

Site: `https://popevents.ca/`

Positioning is corporate event energy at scale, with strong metrics such as events per year, years in business, simultaneous deployments, case studies, blog content, and a clear "Request a Quote" CTA.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- Metrics work. Mahnaz has credible proof numbers and should use them.
- The tone should be more composed and premium than Pop's playful, high-energy positioning.
- Process clarity is valuable, especially if it shows how she prevents event-day problems.

### Executivevents

Site: `https://www.executivevents.com/event-planning/toronto`

Positioning emphasizes conference/event operations, event technology, data-driven success, registration, onsite support, and measurable scale. The site offers a "Free Event Strategy Session" and gives strong proof metrics.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- This is the closest benchmark for the EventAgent bridge.
- The "strategy session" CTA validates the consultation path.
- Mahnaz can credibly connect human event leadership with structured systems, but should keep the personal site simpler than a software/services platform.

### Rafanelli Events

Site: `https://www.rafanellievents.com/`

An aspirational luxury benchmark. The site leads with story, partnership, precision, global reputation, press, and high-end service categories including consulting and branding.

Implication for Mahnaz:

- Story and precision can coexist.
- The site can feel premium without pretending to be a global luxury house.
- Use calm, editorial confidence rather than dense service selling.

### ProVia Events

Site: `https://proviaevents.com/`

ProVia Events already owns the company-service layer: event planning, execution, guest operations, vendor coordination, timelines, and consultation. Mahnaz's website should not duplicate that site. It should answer, "Who is Mahnaz, and why should I trust her judgment?"

### EventAgent

Site: `https://eventagent.io/`

EventAgent's language centers on structure over chaos, centralized coordination, vendors, timelines, contracts, approvals, and real-world execution. This creates a useful strategic bridge for Mahnaz: she can be positioned as someone whose event expertise is grounded not only in hospitality and execution, but also in systems thinking.

## Common Messaging Tropes To Avoid

- "Unforgettable experiences" without explaining what makes the experience work.
- "Flawless execution" as a generic promise.
- "Bespoke luxury" without proof or audience fit.
- Overloaded service menus that make the site feel like a company brochure.
- Vague "we handle every detail" language.
- Personality-only personal branding that does not explain commercial value.
- Event-tech language that makes the site feel like a product page instead of a personal credibility site.

## IA And Funnel Patterns Observed

Common patterns:

- Hero with category claim and primary CTA.
- Service categories by event type.
- Proof strips with metrics, logos, testimonials, or press.
- Portfolio or selected events.
- Process section.
- Blog or guide content for SEO and authority.
- Contact form or booking CTA.

Recommended pattern for Mahnaz:

1. Hero: personal positioning plus consultation CTA.
2. Credibility strip: experience, event scale, ProVia Events, EventAgent, hospitality-tech background.
3. "Where Mahnaz helps" section organized by buyer needs.
4. Selected experience and proof metrics.
5. Operating philosophy: structure, guest experience, vendor alignment, calm execution.
6. ProVia Events and EventAgent context.
7. Final Calendly and LinkedIn CTA.

## Proof And Trust Patterns Observed

Competitors commonly use:

- Client logos.
- Testimonials.
- Press and media.
- Awards.
- Years in business.
- Event volume.
- Case studies.
- Galleries.
- Named high-profile clients or talent.
- Founder story.

Mahnaz has enough proof to compete if it is structured well:

- 10+ years event planning/hospitality experience.
- 18 years total professional experience.
- 100+ events managed independently.
- 800+ attendee and 750+ guest event experience.
- 20+ vendors/sponsors managed on a major event.
- Hospitality technology and 2,000+ client business experience from Banic Software.
- ProVia Events leadership.
- EventAgent strategy connection, pending exact wording.

The biggest proof gap is not substance; it is presentation assets: photo, testimonials, visual event proof, and approved public title wording.

## Visual And UX Patterns Observed

The category splits into three dominant visual modes:

- Luxury/editorial: white space, large imagery, refined typography, elegant portfolios.
- Corporate/agency: service cards, case studies, logos, forms, metrics, content hubs.
- Software/operations: structured diagrams, workflow steps, product language, sharp CTA hierarchy.

Mahnaz's site should blend the first and third lightly:

- Editorial personal credibility.
- Structured operational clarity.
- Warm hospitality tone.
- Minimal service clutter.
- A strong portrait and a few selected proof moments.

Avoid:

- Overly decorative wedding/luxury visuals.
- Loud corporate event-energy design.
- Product-dashboard design that makes the site feel like EventAgent.

## Strategic Whitespace

The strongest whitespace is a personal authority position that says:

- Mahnaz is not just an event planner.
- She is a senior event strategist who understands the room, the guests, the vendors, the timeline, and the operating system behind a successful event.
- Her background bridges hospitality, live-event execution, business development, and event-technology strategy.

Competitors talk about experiences, beauty, logistics, or scale. Fewer clearly own "structured calm under event-day pressure" from a named senior operator.

## Recommendations For Positioning

Stage 03 should explore positioning around:

- Structured event strategy for high-stakes gatherings.
- Calm operational leadership for events that cannot afford confusion.
- Hospitality-informed event execution.
- The bridge between premium guest experience and behind-the-scenes control.
- Personal credibility that strengthens ProVia Events and EventAgent without making the website about either brand.

## Recommendations For Offers

Keep offer framing simple:

- Event Strategy Consultation.
- Corporate and business event advisory.
- Luxury/private event strategy and execution support.
- Hospitality operations consulting.
- Speaking/emcee support as secondary or proof-led.
- EventAgent strategy/operations connection as a credibility layer.

Do not create a broad service catalog yet. That belongs primarily to ProVia Events.

Design should feel like:

Design should not feel like:

- A corporate event agency clone.
- A software product landing page.
- A resume page with decorative sections.
