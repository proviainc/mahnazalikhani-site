# Industry Mode

## Selected Mode

Primary mode: Personal Brand

Supporting modes:

- Consultant
- Local/Premium Service Business
- Enterprise B2B influence for EventAgent and corporate-event credibility

## Rationale

This website is primarily about trust in Mahnaz as a person. Buyers are likely validating her judgment, taste, operational calm, event experience, hospitality knowledge, and ability to protect an important room under pressure.

The Consultant mode matters because the site should make her method, perspective, and fit clear. The Local/Premium Service Business mode matters because many event buyers need simple proof of reliability, service geography, and an easy next action. The Enterprise B2B influence matters where the site supports EventAgent and corporate/business event credibility.

## Mode Implications

### Trust Cues

- Professional portrait
- Concise founder/operator story
- Selected event proof
- Specific attendee/vendor/sponsor counts where verified
- LinkedIn link
- ProVia Events affiliation
- EventAgent strategy affiliation once wording is confirmed
- Hospitality and technology background

### CTA Logic

Primary CTA:

- Book an Event Strategy Consultation

Secondary CTA:

- Connect on LinkedIn

The site should avoid too many parallel CTAs. If speaking, emcee, or consulting inquiries remain important, they can be framed under the same consultation path.

### Structure Emphasis

Recommended early structure:

1. Hero positioning around structured event strategy and execution.
2. Credibility strip with experience, event scale, ProVia Events, EventAgent, and hospitality-tech background.
3. Services/engagement paths by buyer need.
4. Selected experience and notable events.
5. Personal story and operating philosophy.
6. LinkedIn and Calendly CTA.

## Pass / Fail Standard

Pass if the site feels like a credible senior event strategist's personal platform.

Fail if it becomes a generic event-planning service site, a duplicate ProVia Events site, or a thin resume page without a clear buyer action.
