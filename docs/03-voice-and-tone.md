# Stage 03 - Voice And Tone

## Voice Principle

Mahnaz's website should sound like a calm senior event strategist: warm enough for hospitality, precise enough for corporate clients, and grounded enough to be trusted when the event is live.

## Voice Attributes

### Calm

Use composed language that lowers anxiety.

Say:

- Mahnaz helps bring structure to the moving parts behind a successful event.

Avoid:

- Mahnaz makes every event completely stress-free.

### Specific

Name the operational details that matter.

Say:

- Guest arrival, vendor coordination, timeline control, stage flow, and on-site execution.

Avoid:

- Every detail from start to finish.

### Senior

Sound like an experienced operator, not a generic service provider.

Say:

- Senior judgment matters when the room, the timeline, and the guest experience all need to work.

Avoid:

- We are passionate about making dreams come true.

### Warm

Keep the humanity of hospitality present.

Say:

- A well-run event lets hosts stay present with their guests.

Avoid:

- We optimize attendee movement through operational workflows.

### Premium

Premium should mean polished, thoughtful, discreet, and controlled.

Say:

- A polished guest experience supported by clear behind-the-scenes coordination.

Avoid:

- Luxury, bespoke, world-class events for every occasion.

### Practical

Keep claims tied to real work.

Say:

- Mahnaz has managed guest flow, vendor coordination, stage flow, and large-event logistics across corporate, gala, cultural, and private settings.

Avoid:

- Mahnaz creates unforgettable moments that exceed every expectation.

## Preferred Vocabulary

Use:

- Event strategy
- Structured event planning
- Event-day control
- Guest experience
- Guest flow
- Vendor coordination
- Sponsor coordination
- Timeline control
- Program flow
- Stage flow
- Crowd management
- Hospitality judgment
- Live-event execution
- High-stakes gatherings
- Event Strategy Consultation
- Technology-informed event operations

Use carefully:

- Luxury
- Premium
- Full-service
- Production
- Speaking
- Emcee
- EventAgent
- Founder

Avoid:

- Flawless
- Magical
- Stress-free
- Best
- Premier
- World-class
- Bespoke
- Unforgettable as a primary claim
- Every detail

## Tone By Section

### Hero

Confident, concise, and clear. Lead with positioning, not biography.

### Proof Strip

Factual and restrained. Use numbers and roles without hype.

### About

Warm, human, and purposeful. Connect personal journey to present expertise.

### Experience

Specific and evidence-led. Name event types, roles, responsibilities, and scale.

### EventAgent Context

Clear and contained. Explain the event-operations connection without turning the page into a product pitch.

### CTA

Direct and useful.

Example:

- Book an Event Strategy Consultation
- Connect on LinkedIn

## Copy Rules

- Lead with clarity before style.
- Tie claims to proof.
- Separate Mahnaz's personal credibility from ProVia Events service pages.
- Do not use unapproved testimonials or event images.
- Do not overstate EventAgent title until approved.
- Keep SEO phrases natural.
- Treat "premium" as a behavior, not a decoration.
